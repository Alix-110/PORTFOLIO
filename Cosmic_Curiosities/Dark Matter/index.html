Dark Matter: The Invisible Cosmic Skeleton

Dark matter is the name we give to unseen mass that shapes how galaxies rotate, how clusters bind, and how the large-scale cosmic web forms. It doesn't emit or absorb light, but its gravity sculpts the visible universe.

Observations show: galaxies and clusters behave as if a large amount of unseen mass exists. This unseen mass is called dark matter.

Galaxy Clusters

Clusters require more mass than visible matter provides — dark matter supplies the missing gravity.

Rotation Curves

Star speeds far from centers stay high, implying extra invisible mass in halos.

Gravitational Lensing

Light bends more than visible mass can explain; lensing maps reveal dark matter distributions.

What Is Dark Matter? The Hidden 85% of the Universe

Modern cosmology indicates that roughly 85% of the matter in the universe is dark. Ordinary atoms make up only ~15% of matter. The rest is invisible to telescopes.

Evidence: rotation curves, galaxy motions in clusters, gravitational lensing, the cosmic microwave background (CMB), and structure formation all point to non-luminous mass.

Scientists infer dark matter from gravitational effects. It is essential in models that form galaxies quickly enough to match observations.

  • CMB: tiny fluctuations encode matter content.
  • Large-scale structure: simulations with dark matter reproduce the cosmic web.
  • Direct searches: experiments hunt for WIMPs, axions, and other candidates.

The Universe’s Shadow: Understanding Dark Matter

Multiple independent observations converge on the dark matter picture. The 'shadow' of dark matter appears in many datasets.

Cosmic Microwave Background

Small CMB fluctuations encode the Universe’s matter content; analysis requires dark matter to match data.

Lensing Maps

By mapping distortions we reveal mass maps that highlight dark halos even where there is no light.

Structure Formation

Simulations that include dark matter reproduce the cosmic web and galaxy clustering we observe.

Dark Matter — The Hidden Framework Holding Everything Together

On the largest scales dark matter forms filaments and nodes — the cosmic web. Galaxies form inside halos sitting on these filaments.
