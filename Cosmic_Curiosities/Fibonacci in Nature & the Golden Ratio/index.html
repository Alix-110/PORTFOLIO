Fibonacci & The Golden Ratio

Explore how nature, art, and the cosmos follow a hidden pattern—the Fibonacci sequence and the Golden Ratio.

What is the Fibonacci Sequence?

The Fibonacci sequence is a series of numbers where each number is the sum of the two preceding ones: 0, 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21...

It’s closely related to the Golden Ratio (≈ 1.618), which describes proportional harmony in nature, architecture, and art.

Fibonacci in Nature

  • Sunflowers: The seeds grow in spirals that follow Fibonacci numbers to maximize packing efficiency.
  • Pinecones: Spirals on pinecones follow Fibonacci numbers.
  • Shells & Nautilus: Shells often form logarithmic spirals approximating the Golden Ratio.
  • Leaves & Petals: Phyllotaxis (arrangement of leaves) follows Fibonacci numbers for optimal sunlight exposure.

Fun & Mind-Blowing Facts About Fibonacci

Pinecone Spirals

Count the spirals on a pinecone—most follow Fibonacci numbers.

Nautilus Shell

The logarithmic spiral of shells approximates the Golden Ratio for optimal growth.

Petals

Many flowers have petals in Fibonacci numbers (3, 5, 8, 13, 21).

Pineapple Spirals

The scales of a pineapple follow multiple Fibonacci spirals in different directions.

Galaxies

Spiral galaxies often form patterns related to the Fibonacci spiral.

Fingerprints

The whorls in fingerprints sometimes follow Fibonacci or Golden Ratio patterns.

Art & Architecture

Many famous artworks and buildings use proportions approximating the Golden Ratio.

Human Body

Some body proportions approximate the Golden Ratio, like the ratio of forearm to hand.

Fibonacci in the Cosmos

Even space isn’t free from Fibonacci patterns:

  • Spiral galaxies often approximate logarithmic spirals based on Fibonacci numbers.
  • Planetary ring patterns sometimes show distribution ratios close to Fibonacci numbers.
  • Simulations of cosmic structures reveal fractal-like patterns that connect to Fibonacci sequences.
